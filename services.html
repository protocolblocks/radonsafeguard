Residential

Home Radon Testing

Every Alberta home should be tested for radon. Our C-NRPP-certified technicians use Health Canada–aligned continuous radon monitors (CRMs) and charcoal canister tests to deliver accurate, defensible results for homeowners, buyers, and sellers.

We follow strict closed-house conditions and place devices in the lowest occupied level of your home for the most reliable readings.

  • 48-hour continuous monitor testing with hourly data
  • Long-term 90-day alpha track testing available
  • Tamper-proof seals and chain-of-custody documentation
  • Results delivered within 24 hours of test completion

Mitigation

Radon Mitigation Systems

When test results exceed 200 Bq/m³, a professionally designed mitigation system is the proven solution. We install active soil depressurization (ASD) systems tailored to your building's foundation type, layout, and radon levels.

Every installation includes a post-mitigation test to verify radon levels have been reduced below the Health Canada guideline — backed by our 5-year workmanship warranty.

  • Sub-slab depressurization for basements & slab-on-grade
  • Crawl space sub-membrane depressurization
  • Low-profile, quiet fan systems with visual gauges
  • Post-mitigation verification testing included

Maintenance

Maintenance of Existing Systems

Even the best-designed mitigation systems need ongoing care. As buildings settle, materials age, and new cracks form, systems can gradually lose efficiency — allowing radon levels to creep back up over the years.

Our maintenance programs are designed to keep your system operating at peak performance, ensure radon levels remain safely low, extend equipment life, and protect your original investment in a healthier home or building.

  • Annual or bi-annual full system inspections and performance testing
  • Fan and motor replacement (the most critical component)
  • Re-sealing cracks, joints, and penetrations caused by settling
  • Noise reduction upgrades: silencers, vibration isolators, and relocated exhausts
  • Piping inspection, clearing blockages, and component upgrades
  • Post-maintenance verification testing and detailed reporting
  • Smart monitoring upgrades and alarm calibration

How It Works

Radon Mitigation Explained

Active soil depressurization is the most effective method for reducing radon in Alberta homes.

1. Radon Enters

Radon gas rises from uranium in soil and seeps through your foundation, cracks, and utility gaps.

2. System Installed

A small hole is drilled in the foundation slab and connected to PVC piping routed to a quiet fan.

3. Fan Draws Gas

The fan creates negative pressure beneath the slab, pulling radon from the soil before it enters your home.

4. Safe Air

Radon is vented above the roofline and dissipates harmlessly. Post-mitigation testing confirms success.
