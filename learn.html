Expert answers and resources about radon testing, mitigation, and safety for Edmonton and Alberta homeowners.

General Questions

Radon is a colorless, odorless radioactive gas that forms naturally when uranium breaks down in soil and rock. It can seep into buildings through foundation cracks, gaps around pipes, and other openings. Health Canada estimates radon causes roughly 16% of lung cancer deaths in Canada, making it the leading cause of lung cancer in non-smokers.

Alberta has some of the highest indoor radon levels in Canada, with many Edmonton-area homes exceeding Health Canada's guideline of 200 Bq/m³. Levels vary significantly by neighbourhood, geology, and even from house to house on the same street. The only way to know your risk is to test.

Radon is typically highest on the lowest occupied level of a building, but elevated levels can be found on upper floors as well — especially in homes with HVAC systems that circulate air between floors. Slab-on-grade homes without basements can still have high radon levels. Testing should always be performed on the lowest lived-in level.

Testing

Our standard short-term test runs for 48 hours using a continuous radon monitor. For real estate transactions, this is the most common approach and delivers results within 24 hours of test completion. Long-term tests (90 days or more) are also available for a more accurate annual average reading.

Health Canada recommends taking action to reduce radon when levels reach 200 becquerels per cubic metre (Bq/m³) or higher. The World Health Organization recommends action at 100 Bq/m³. Even levels below 200 Bq/m³ carry some risk, and many Edmonton homeowners choose to mitigate at lower levels for added peace of mind.

For accurate results, maintain closed-house conditions for 12 hours before and during the test. This means keeping windows and exterior doors closed (normal entry/exit is fine). Avoid operating whole-house fans, and do not test during severe storms. Our technician will walk you through all requirements at scheduling.

Health Canada recommends testing every two years, even if you previously had low results. Retest after any major renovation, foundation work, or HVAC changes. If you have a mitigation system installed, we recommend testing every two years to confirm the system continues to operate effectively.

Mitigation

The most common method is active soil depressurization (ASD). A fan draws radon gas from beneath your foundation through PVC piping and vents it safely above the roofline, where it dissipates harmlessly into the atmosphere. This creates negative pressure under the slab, preventing radon from entering your home. Systems typically reduce radon levels by 80–99%.

Pricing is determined by the complexity of each individual job. Factors include your home's foundation type, building size, radon levels, crawl space conditions, and the mitigation system design required. We provide a detailed written quote after assessing your property — at no obligation.

Modern mitigation systems are designed to be as unobtrusive as possible. PVC piping is typically routed along the exterior of the home or through a garage or closet. The fan is mounted in the attic, garage, or outside, and operates quietly — usually no louder than a refrigerator. Most homeowners barely notice it running.

Mitigation systems require minimal maintenance. Check the U-tube manometer (visual gauge) monthly to confirm the fan is running. Fans typically last 5–10 years before needing replacement. We recommend a professional inspection every two years and include post-mitigation verification testing with every installation.

Real Estate & Commercial

Radon testing is not legally required in Alberta, but it is strongly recommended by Health Canada and most home inspectors during real estate transactions. Because radon is invisible and undetectable without professional equipment, testing is standard practice in the Edmonton market. We offer expedited 48-hour testing designed for closing timelines.

This is negotiable between buyer and seller, similar to other repair items. In many markets, the seller installs mitigation before closing if elevated levels are found. In others, buyers negotiate a credit. We provide same-day mitigation quotes to help keep transactions on track, and we work directly with agents, attorneys, and lenders.

Yes. Health Canada recommends that all buildings — including offices, schools, and multi-unit properties — be tested for radon. Under Alberta's Occupational Health and Safety Code, employers and property managers have a duty to provide a safe environment for occupants. We offer customized multi-zone testing plans with compliance documentation for commercial clients.
